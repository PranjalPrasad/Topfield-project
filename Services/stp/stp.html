About STP

What is a Sewage
Treatment Plant?

A Sewage Treatment Plant (STP) is a facility designed to remove contaminants from wastewater generated by homes, commercial complexes and industries before it is safely discharged or reused.

At Topfield Water Solutions, we design and commission STP plants using MBR (Membrane BioReactor) technology as well as conventional SBR and MBBR processes — available in both civil construction and MS fabricated formats.

Our plants comply fully with CPCB & MPCB norms, and are used across Pune's leading housing societies, hospitals and commercial complexes.

Treatment Process

How Sewage Becomes
Clean Water

A multi-stage biological and physical process that transforms raw sewage into treated water compliant with discharge norms.

01

Raw Sewage Inlet

Wastewater enters the plant from building drains & collection network.

02

Screening

Coarse & fine screens remove solids, rags and debris from raw sewage.

03

Aeration Tank

Aerobic bacteria digest organic matter in the aeration chamber.

04

Clarifier / Membrane

Solids are separated from treated liquid via sedimentation or membranes.

05

Treated Water Out

Safe, clean effluent ready for discharge or reuse as per norms.
